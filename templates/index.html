About the Open Journal Club

The Open Journal Club is an experimetal literature discussion forum. Its aim is to develop literature mining and critical discussion skills.

The Open Journal Club is a webapp powered by Google App Engine and Crocodoc. For reasons of Internet security, you need to login through a Google account. If you prefer to use a Yahoo account you can couple it to a Google account in a relatively painless way.

The Open Journal Club consists of discussion threads, each attempting to answer a single question. As a participant, you can leave comments and annotate embedded scientific papers. You can also upload pdf documents and propose your own topic of discussion, by .

A good place to get started with the Open Journal Club is the didactic thread How to use the Open Journal Club? It is meant as a practice and testing place, so feel free to leave comments of any kind and practice annotating pdf documents in place. Crocodoc, the engine used to annotate pdf documents still has a few bugs. In particular, undoing the annotations can sometimes cause the page to crash. That's unfortunate, but there is not much I can do about it. In any event, you can always download the pdf documents.

The Open Journal Club is written in Python and is open source. Please don't hesitate to fork, copy or steal the code from Github.

As stated in the introduction, the Open Journal Club is an experiment (technically as much as scientifically). We want to learn how to efficiently promote scientific dscussion and critical thinking and we want to improve. So don't hesitate to give some feedback or ask for support, by .
